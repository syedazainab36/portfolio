LATH MACHINE

A lathe is a machine tool that rotates a workpiece about an axis of rotation to perform various operations, such as cutting, sanding, knurling, drilling, deformation, facing, and turning, with tools that are applied to the workpiece to create an object with symmetry about that axis.

Lathe machines come in various sizes and configurations, from small bench lathes used for hobbyists and small workshops to large industrial lathes capable of handling heavy-duty machining. The type of lathe and its features depend on the specific machining requirements.

Operators use lathes to create cylindrical parts, turn metal, wood, or other materials, and produce objects with a variety of shapes and sizes. CNC (Computer Numerical Control) lathes have become increasingly common, allowing for automated and precise control of the machining process.
